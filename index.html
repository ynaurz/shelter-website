What is the process for adopting a dog?

The adoption process typically involves filling out an application, meeting with a shelter counselor to find a suitable match, and spending time with potential dogs. Once a match is found, there's usually a home visit and an adoption fee, which covers vaccinations, spay/neuter, and microchipping.

How can I tell if a dog is right for my family?

We recommend considering the dog's temperament, energy level, size, and how well these traits match your family's lifestyle. Interacting with the dog multiple times and discussing its history and behavior with shelter staff can help you make an informed decision.

Are all the dogs at the shelter vaccinated and neutered?

Yes, all dogs available for adoption have been vaccinated, spayed or neutered, and received a general health check. We also provide you with a medical history report at the time of adoption.

Can I volunteer at the shelter? What would I do?

Volunteers are always welcome! Typical tasks include walking dogs, assisting with feedings, helping during adoption events, and providing basic training and socialization. To start volunteering, you'll need to attend a brief orientation session.

What should I bring when I come to adopt a dog?

You should bring a valid ID, proof of address, and a form of payment for the adoption fee. It’s also a good idea to bring a leash and collar, but these are available for purchase at the shelter if needed.
